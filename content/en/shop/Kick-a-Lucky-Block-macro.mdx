<Section icon={Target} title="Setting Up Your Kick a Lucky Block Macro">

To effectively use a Kick a Lucky Block macro, you need a reliable tool. While many players use basic auto-clickers, tools like TinyTask or specialized macro recorders allow for movement recording. This is necessary because the game requires you to run back to your plot to "bank" the Brainrot earned from a kick. Without the return trip, the tsunami will eventually catch you, and you will lose the reward.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Positioning Your Character">
    Stand in the exact center of your plot, facing the Lucky Block. Ensure your camera is locked or set to a consistent angle to prevent the macro from drifting over time.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Recording the Training Phase">
    If your goal is Kick Power, record yourself clicking with your best weight equipped. Most weights, like the Bone Barbell or Copper Plate, require consistent clicking to generate power.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Recording the Farming Loop">
    Record the following sequence: Kick the block -> Wait for the landing -> Run back to the plot immediately -> Wait 2 seconds for the Brainrot to register -> Repeat.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Testing for Tsunami Timing">
    Play back your recording. If the tsunami catches you during the playback, you need to either increase your Run Speed or shorten the wait time in your macro.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| | | | |
| OP AutoClicker | Rapid clicking | Weight Training | Windows/Mac |
| TinyTask | Movement recording | Full Farming Loops | Windows |
