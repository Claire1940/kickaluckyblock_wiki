## Essential Glitches and Movement Tech

To collect a **kick a lucky block rare brainrot** from the furthest zones, such as the Celestial area, you must contend with the "Wave." This tsunami-like barrier chases you back to the lobby after you kick a block. If the wave touches you, you lose the unit you just uncovered.

### The Floating Glitch
A popular, albeit mostly cosmetic, glitch involves the weight-lifting mechanic. By jumping and simultaneously activating your weight-lifting tool, your character can enter a "floating" state. While this doesn't directly help you outrun the wave, it can be used to position yourself more effectively before a kick or to avoid ground-based obstacles in the lobby.

### Skipping the Wave (Wi-Fi Trick)
There is a high-risk technique involving network manipulation to bypass the wave. By temporarily disabling your internet connection (turning off Wi-Fi) just as the wave approaches, the game client may fail to register the "kill" hit. If you turn the Wi-Fi back on after the wave has passed your character model, you may find yourself still alive.

> **💡 Tip:** This glitch is extremely inconsistent and depends heavily on server latency. Developers frequently patch network-based exploits, so do not rely on this for your most valuable units.

### The Tab-Out Method (Mobile Only)
On mobile devices, some players have reported success by "holding the tab" (opening the app switcher) without fully closing the Roblox app. This pauses the client-side simulation of the wave. If timed correctly, you can wait for the server-side wave to dissipate before returning to the game. However, like the Wi-Fi trick, this is often patched in newer versions of the [official Roblox platform](https://www.roblox.com/games/).
