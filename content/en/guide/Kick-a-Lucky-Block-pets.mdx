## Understanding the "Brain Rot" Pet System

The core gameplay loop involves kicking a lucky block as far as possible. The distance the block travels determines the quality of the "Brain Rot" pet you receive. However, obtaining **Kick a Lucky Block pets** isn't as simple as just opening a crate. You must physically run to the location where the block landed, claim the pet, and then race back to your base before a deadly wave catches you.

If the wave hits you before you reach the safety of the starting zone, you lose the pet entirely. This creates a high-stakes environment where greed can lead to total loss. Your pets are then placed on your base floors to generate income every second.

## Balancing Kick Power and Run Speed

A common mistake among new players is focusing entirely on Kick Power. While high power allows you to reach better **Kick a Lucky Block pets** further down the track, it is useless if you aren't fast enough to outrun the wave.

1.  **Kick Power (Weights):** Increases the distance the block travels. Higher distance = Higher rarity potential.
2.  **Run Speed (Blue Stand):** Increases your movement velocity. Essential for returning to base with your pet.
3.  **The Perfect Score:** Timing your kick to hit the "Tippy Top" of the charge bar provides a massive distance boost regardless of your current strength.

## Rebirthing and Long-term Scaling

The rebirth system is the backbone of the 2026 meta. Rebirthing resets your Kick Power to zero but provides a permanent money multiplier. This multiplier applies to all the cash generated by your **Kick a Lucky Block pets**. 

Before you rebirth, ensure you have a full roster of high-level pets. Since your kick power resets, you won't be able to get high-tier pets for a while after rebirthing. Having a solid "bank" of pets generating billions per second will allow you to buy back your weights and speed upgrades almost instantly.

> **Warning:** Never rebirth if you have an empty base. You will find it extremely difficult to progress back to your previous strength without the passive income from your Brain Rots.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I get more slots for my Kick a Lucky Block pets?**

A: You can increase your pet capacity by purchasing additional floors for your base. Currently, the game allows for three floors, totaling 30 pet slots. Always upgrade your floors as soon as you have the cash to do so.

**Q: Why did I lose my pet after kicking the block?**

A: You likely got caught by the wave. When you kick a block, you must run to it, pick up the Brain Rot, and return to the starting line before the wave reaches you. If the wave touches you, the pet is lost.

**Q: What is the max level for pets in Kick a Lucky Block?**

A: As of the 2026 updates, the maximum level for any pet (Brain Rot) is level 75. Reaching this level requires a significant amount of cash but drastically increases the pet's income per second.

**Q: Is it better to have high Kick Power or high Speed?**

A: You need a balance. High Kick Power gets you better pets, but high Speed ensures you actually keep them. A good rule of thumb is to spend 60% of your resources on Power and 40% on Speed.
